Fundamentals vs Ideal

Green = in the ideal zone, red = well outside it, grey = in between or n/a. Requires a Finnhub key (🔑 API keys above). Speculative growth names will run red on earnings metrics — that's the trade-off: you're paying for future growth, not current profits.

📖 Cheat Sheet — How to Read a Stock

MetricIdeal zoneWhat it tells you
EPS (earnings per share)+15% per yearProfit ÷ share count. Two checks, in plain English: (1) Is EPS at least 15% higher than last year? Example: $2.00 last year → want $2.30+ this year. It's about the trend, NOT a % of the stock price. (2) Is EPS at least 1/25th of the share price? $100 stock → want $4+ EPS. $50 stock → $2+. Both boxes ticked = healthy. EPS below zero = company loses money; skip this row and check cash runway instead.
P/E (price ÷ EPS)15–25Years of current earnings you're paying for. <15 cheap (or market smells trouble), >40 means big growth is already priced in. Meaningless when EPS is negative.
Forward P/E10–20Same ratio on next year's expected earnings. Lower than trailing = analysts expect profits to grow.
PEG (P/E ÷ growth rate)0–1P/E adjusted for growth. Under 1 = growth is cheap; over 2 = you're overpaying for it. Best single ratio for growth stocks.
P/S (price ÷ sales)0–10 (best < 2)The go-to for unprofitable companies. Quantum names trade at 100x+ — that's pure story premium.
EV (enterprise value)0.8–1.0x market capMarket cap + debt − cash: the true takeover price. EV well below market cap = big cash pile (safety); well above = debt-heavy.
EV/Sales0–10 (best < 3)Like P/S but debt- and cash-adjusted, so it's fairer when comparing companies with different balance sheets.
EBITDAMargin ≥ 15%Rough operating cash profit (before interest, tax, depreciation). First profitability milestone a speculative company hits.
EV/EBITDA8–15Preferred takeover math. <8 = cheap, >20 = expensive. Only meaningful once EBITDA is positive.
P/B (price ÷ book value)1–3Price vs net assets. Matters for banks/industrials/miners; mostly ignore for software and story stocks.
Profit margin≥ 10%Cents of profit per $1 of sales. Negative = burning cash → check dilution and cash runway next.
ROE (return on equity)15–25%How hard shareholder money works. Consistently high ROE = quality compounder.
Beta0.8–1.2Volatility vs the S&P. High-beta names swing 2-3x the market's moves, both directions.
% off 52-wk high0% to −15%Near highs = strength (momentum). More than 50% below = broken chart — falling-knife territory unless the story changed.
Share count trend≤ 0%/yr (max +5%)The silent killer for speculative names. Growing 20%+/yr means your slice shrinks even if the company wins.
Rule of thumb for speculative names: earnings metrics will mostly be red, so judge on P/S or EV/Sales vs growth rate, cash runway (cash ÷ yearly burn — want 2+ years), dilution pace, and whether EBITDA is trending toward positive.
